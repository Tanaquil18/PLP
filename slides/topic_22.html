Topic 22—Subsets and power sets

Demirbaş & Rechnitzer

Subsets

Doing more with sets

Is the set \(A\) contained in the set \(B\text{?}\)

Definition: Subset.

Let \(A,B\) be sets

  • We say that \(A\) is a subset of \(B\) when every element of \(A\) is also an element of \(B\text{.}\)
  • We denote this \(A \subseteq B\) and also call \(B\) a superset of \(A\text{.}\) We can also write \(B \supseteq A\text{.}\)
  • \(A\) is a proper subset of \(B\) when \(A \subseteq B\text{,}\) but \(B\) contains at least one element that is not in \(A\text{.}\)

  • Finally, two \(A\) and \(B\) are equal when they are subsets of each other. That is

    \begin{equation*} A = B \iff \left( (A\subseteq B) \land (B \subseteq A) \right) \end{equation*}

Notes and examples

Note that

  • For all sets \(A\text{,}\) \(\es \subseteq A\) and \(A \subseteq A\)
  • \(\displaystyle A \subseteq B \quad \equiv \quad \forall a \in A, a \in B \quad \equiv \quad (a \in A) \implies (a \in B)\)
  • \(\displaystyle A \not\subseteq B \quad \equiv \quad \exists a \in A \;s.t.\; a \not\in B\)

Some examples

  • \(\displaystyle \set{1,2,7} \not\subseteq \set{1,2,3,4,5}\)
  • \(\displaystyle \set{2n \st n \in \mathbb{Z}} \subseteq \mathbb{Z}\)
  • The subsets of \(\set{0,1}\) are \(\es, \set{0}, \set{1}, \set{0,1}\)

The set of all subsets

Definition:

Let \(A\) be a set. The power set of \(A\text{,}\) denoted \(\mathcal{P}(A)\text{,}\) is the set of all subsets of \(A\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \pow{\es} \amp = \set{\es} \\ \pow{\set{1}} \amp = \set{\es, \set{1}} \\ \pow{\set{0,1}}\amp= \set{ \es, \set{0}, \set{1}, \set{0,1}} \\ \pow{\set{0,1,2}}\amp= \set{ \es, \set{0}, \set{1}, \set{2},\set{0,1}, \set{0,2}, \set{1,2}, \set{0,1,2}} \end{align*}
Not hard to prove that if \(|A|=n\) then \(|\pow{A}| = 2^n\text{.}\)
